The nice thing about beginning hiking is that you don't really need any special gear, you can probably get away with things you already have. Let's start with clothing — a typical mistake beginners make is wearing jeans, which get heavy and chafe if they get sweaty or wet.

Understand Your Map & Timing

To start, print out the hiking guide and map. If it's raining, throw them in a zip-lock bag. Read over the guide, study the map, and have a good idea of what to expect. Know what your next landmark is as you hike and always track your timing carefully.
